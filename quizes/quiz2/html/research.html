My Research

As a Graduate Research Assistant, I have been developing a perception- based algorithm that allows a robot to avoid collision by detecting, localizing, and estimating the motion of humans while navigating in a construction site (dynamic environment). I use formal specification language (Signal Tempo ral Logic) to specify the safety requirements for the robot and control its mo tion. I have developed a sound knowledge of Signal Temporal Logic which can be applied to various domains such as safe reinforcement learning and model predictive control. Additionally, I am skilled in coding with ROS (Robot Op erating System), modeling simulations in Gazebo, and visualization tools like RVIZ (ROS Visualization). I have worked on various deep-learning projects involving a variety of neural network architectures such as UNET, GRU (Gated Recurrent Unit), LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory), CNN (convolutional neural networks), transformers, etc. During my undergraduate studies, I have been engaged in diverse projects encompassing embedded systems, the Internet of Things (IoT), and deep learning. These projects resulted in publications ac cepted by IEEE conferences, highlighting their practical significance. Through out the academic journey, I have cultivated essential soft skills such as effective communication, problem-solving, time management, and adaptability, which are invaluable in academia as well as in various professional and personal endeavors.
